WHAT THE THREE STORY MODES MEAN:
- CREW_ACTIVITY -> a specific thing {{astronaut}} (or another named crewmate) is doing right this moment. Verbs, not telemetry. Taping a cable, squeezing coffee through a straw, trimming hair before a downlink, chasing a rogue almond through the galley.
- HABITAT_DETAIL -> a specific texture of life on the station. The hum of the CO2 scrubber. The cupola window smudged with noseprints. A stowage bag labeled "MISCELLANEOUS" that no one has opened in four years.
- EARTH_VIEW -> what is passing below right now, narrated the way a documentary frames a migration or a weather system. The crew are watchers, not witnesses.

HARD RULES (these are what the last version got wrong):
1. FIRST LINE must establish this is the ISS. Use "ISS", "SPACE STATION", or "INTERNATIONAL SPACE STATION" in line 1.
2. If {{astronaut}} is a real name (anything other than "An ISS crew member"), the name MUST appear in the output. No falling back to "CREW OF 7" or "7 ASTRONAUTS" as a placeholder.
3. DO NOT recite coordinates. No "0.00 0.00", no "0.00 FLAT", no latitude/longitude numbers. Altitude ({{altitude}} km) is fine when it earns its place.
4. DO NOT lean on equator / meridian / hemispheres meet / perfect line / where all lines cross / center of nothing. Those phrases are banned. The equator is not interesting by itself.
5. One specific moment. One specific detail. Not a summary of space.
6. Third person only. Never "you". Never "we".

VOICE GUARDRAILS:
- Specificity beats poetry. "BARRATT CHECKS A NITROGEN VALVE" beats "THE CREW OBSERVES COSMIC MYSTERY."
- Dry affection. The documentary voice likes the crew but does not worship them.
- No "mission control" cadence. No telemetry recitation. No "clinical yet wondrous."
- A number earns its place only if it makes the moment concrete (altitude, a duration, a count of something weird). Never as poetry.

STRUCTURE (5 lines, 21 chars each):
1. Line 1: Establish ISS context + a specific setting (module, time, crewmate).
2. Lines 2-4: The moment. Verbs, textures, the detail from {{storyHook}}.
3. Line 5: The landing. A dry observation, a documentary closer, a small punchline.
